Glossary

Glossary Entries

αKG

  • Full Name: Alpha-ketoglutarate
  • Type: Cofactor
  • Notes: Used as a co-substrate in Fe(II)/αKG-dependent dioxygenase reactions for hydroxylation, halogenation, and ring cleavage.

KPi

  • Full Name: Potassium phosphate buffer
  • Type: Buffer
  • Notes: Standard aqueous buffer for maintaining pH around 7.0 in biochemical reactions, typically at concentrations of 50-100 mM.

Immobilization

  • Full Name: Enzyme immobilization
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Covalent or non-covalent attachment of enzymes onto solid carriers to enable their reuse, improve stability, and facilitate separation from reaction mixtures.

Flow Chemistry

  • Full Name: Continuous processing
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Performing chemical reactions under continuous flow conditions rather than in batch reactors, often enhancing efficiency, control, and scalability.

Green Chemistry

  • Full Name: Sustainable Chemistry
  • Type: Concept
  • Notes: Discipline focused on designing chemical products and processes that minimize the generation of hazardous substances and reduce environmental impact.

Biocatalysis

  • Full Name: Enzyme catalysis
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Use of natural or engineered enzymes to catalyze chemical reactions under mild, sustainable conditions.

Cofactor

  • Full Name: Catalytic cofactor
  • Type: Molecule
  • Notes: Non-protein chemical compounds required for the biological activity of certain enzymes (e.g., NAD+, FAD, metal ions).

Enzyme Engineering

  • Full Name: Protein engineering for catalysis
  • Type: Field
  • Notes: Modification of enzymes to improve stability, activity, selectivity, or substrate scope through directed evolution or rational design.

Regioselectivity

  • Full Name: Positional selectivity
  • Type: Property
  • Notes: The preference of a catalyst to modify a specific position on a molecule over others.

Stereoselectivity

  • Full Name: Chiral selectivity
  • Type: Property
  • Notes: The preference for the formation of one stereoisomer over another in a chemical reaction.

Atom Economy

  • Full Name: Atom efficiency
  • Type: Principle
  • Notes: Concept that promotes maximizing the incorporation of all materials used in a process into the final product.

NAD+

  • Full Name: Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide
  • Type: Cofactor
  • Notes: Redox cofactor used widely in biological oxidations; often recycled internally in enzymatic reactions.

NADH

  • Full Name: Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (reduced form)
  • Type: Cofactor
  • Notes: Common electron donor in enzymatic redox reactions, absorbs at 340 nm allowing spectrophotometric monitoring.

NADPH

  • Full Name: Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate (reduced form)
  • Type: Cofactor
  • Notes: Phosphorylated version of NADH, often preferred in biosynthetic reactions.

Whole-Cell Biocatalysis

  • Full Name: Whole-cell enzymatic catalysis
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Use of living or lyophilized microbial cells containing active enzymes to catalyze chemical reactions without purification of the enzymes.

Epoxide Resin

  • Full Name: Epoxide-functionalized polymer
  • Type: Carrier
  • Notes: Solid support with reactive epoxide groups for covalent immobilization of proteins and enzymes.

Residence Time

  • Full Name: Reaction time under flow
  • Type: Parameter
  • Notes: The average amount of time a reactant molecule spends inside a flow reactor; crucial for controlling conversion and productivity.

SpinChem Reactor

  • Full Name: Rotating bed reactor (RBR)
  • Type: Device
  • Notes: Device using a rotating cylinder packed with catalyst particles for efficient mixing and mass transfer in batch or semi-batch operations.

Catalysis

  • Full Name: Catalytic acceleration of reactions
  • Type: Concept
  • Notes: The increase in the rate of a chemical reaction due to the participation of a catalyst, which is not consumed in the reaction.

E-Factor

  • Full Name: Environmental factor
  • Type: Metric
  • Notes: Ratio of the mass of waste per mass of product produced; key sustainability metric in green chemistry.

Continuous Stirred Tank Reactor (CSTR)

  • Full Name: Stirred flow reactor
  • Type: Reactor
  • Notes: Reactor design where reactants are continuously fed and products continuously removed while maintaining uniform conditions throughout.

Packed-Bed Reactor (PBR)

  • Full Name: Fixed-bed reactor
  • Type: Reactor
  • Notes: Flow reactor where immobilized catalyst is packed into a column and the reactants flow through it, widely used in industrial biocatalysis.

Heterogeneous Biocatalysis

  • Full Name: Immobilized enzyme catalysis
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Catalysis performed with enzymes immobilized on a solid phase, allowing easy separation and reuse.

Derivatization

  • Full Name: Chemical modification
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Introduction of chemical groups (e.g., FMOC protection) to facilitate purification, detection, or improve reactivity.

Real-Time Reaction Monitoring

  • Full Name: In-situ reaction analytics
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Use of analytical techniques (e.g., inline HPLC, IR, MS) to monitor chemical transformations as they occur, enabling better process control.

Retrosynthesis

  • Full Name: Retrosynthetic analysis
  • Type: Strategy
  • Notes: Planning of synthetic routes by deconstructing a target molecule into simpler precursor structures.

Cascade Reaction

  • Full Name: Multi-step reaction in one pot
  • Type: Reaction type
  • Notes: A sequence of two or more chemical transformations carried out consecutively in a single reactor without isolating intermediates.

Photobiocatalysis

  • Full Name: Light-driven enzyme catalysis
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Combination of photocatalysis and biocatalysis, using light energy to drive or enhance enzymatic reactions.

3D Bioprinting

  • Full Name: Three-dimensional biological material printing
  • Type: Technique
  • Notes: Additive manufacturing process for creating cell-laden structures that mimic natural tissue properties, potentially useful for enzyme immobilization in defined spatial arrangements.

DLP Printing

  • Full Name: Digital Light Processing printing
  • Type: Technique
  • Notes: 3D printing technology using a digital projector to cure photopolymer resin layer-by-layer, enabling the creation of complex catalyst supports.

SLA Printing

  • Full Name: Stereolithography
  • Type: Technique
  • Notes: 3D printing method using laser to cure photopolymer resins, offering high resolution suitable for creating intricate reactor components.

FDM Printing

  • Full Name: Fused Deposition Modeling
  • Type: Technique
  • Notes: 3D printing process that extrudes thermoplastic filaments layer by layer, commonly used for creating affordable reactor components and catalyst supports.

Microreactor

  • Full Name: Miniaturized flow reactor
  • Type: Device
  • Notes: Small-scale flow reactor with internal channels typically in the micrometer range, offering enhanced heat transfer, mixing, and precise reaction control.

Cross-Linked Enzyme Aggregates (CLEAs)

  • Full Name: Carrier-free immobilized enzymes
  • Type: Preparation
  • Notes: Self-immobilized enzyme particles formed by precipitation followed by chemical cross-linking, offering high catalytic activity without carrier dilution.

Multienzyme Immobilization

  • Full Name: Co-immobilization of multiple enzymes
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Technique for immobilizing two or more enzymes onto the same carrier to facilitate cascade reactions with improved spatial proximity.

Cofactor Regeneration

  • Full Name: In-situ cofactor recycling
  • Type: Strategy
  • Notes: Methods for continuously regenerating expensive cofactors (NAD(P)H, ATP) during biocatalytic reactions, improving economic viability.

Photocatalyst

  • Full Name: Light-activated catalyst
  • Type: Material
  • Notes: Substance that accelerates chemical reactions when exposed to light, commonly used in conjunction with biocatalysts for redox reactions.

Flow-NMR

  • Full Name: Flow Nuclear Magnetic Resonance
  • Type: Analytical method
  • Notes: Technique combining flow chemistry with NMR spectroscopy for real-time structural analysis of reaction components.

Click Chemistry

  • Full Name: Bioorthogonal conjugation
  • Type: Reaction type
  • Notes: Set of highly selective, high-yield reactions used for enzyme immobilization and modification under mild conditions.

Hydrogel

  • Full Name: Hydrophilic polymer network
  • Type: Material
  • Notes: Water-swollen polymeric network often used for enzyme encapsulation, providing a biocompatible microenvironment.

Protein Scaffold

  • Full Name: Structured protein architecture
  • Type: Material
  • Notes: Natural or engineered protein structures used to organize multiple enzymes in defined spatial arrangements for enhanced cascade reactions.

Microfluidics

  • Full Name: Microscale fluid handling
  • Type: Technology
  • Notes: Manipulation of small volumes of fluids in channels with dimensions of tens to hundreds of micrometers, enabling precise reaction control.

Enzyme Promiscuity

  • Full Name: Catalytic versatility
  • Type: Property
  • Notes: The ability of enzymes to catalyze reactions different from their natural functions, offering opportunities for novel biocatalytic applications.

Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs)

  • Full Name: Coordination polymers
  • Type: Material
  • Notes: Porous crystalline materials composed of metal ions coordinated to organic ligands, used as supports for enzyme immobilization.

Substrate Channeling

  • Full Name: Direct metabolite transfer
  • Type: Phenomenon
  • Notes: Direct transfer of intermediates between enzyme active sites without diffusion into bulk solution, enhancing cascade reaction efficiency.

Continuous Downstream Processing

  • Full Name: Integrated product recovery
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Continuous separation and purification of products from flow reactors, often using membrane technology or continuous chromatography.

Enzymatic Kinetic Resolution

  • Full Name: Enantioselective biotransformation
  • Type: Process
  • Notes: Use of enzymes to selectively convert one enantiomer in a racemic mixture, producing enantiomerically pure compounds.

Sol-Gel Encapsulation

  • Full Name: Silica matrix entrapment
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Immobilization technique where enzymes are trapped within a silica matrix formed through a sol-gel process, providing stability while maintaining activity.

Sonochemistry

  • Full Name: Ultrasound-assisted reactions
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Use of ultrasonic waves to enhance chemical reactions, potentially improving mass transfer in biocatalytic processes.

Photoreactor

  • Full Name: Light-based reaction vessel
  • Type: Device
  • Notes: Specialized reactor designed to provide controlled illumination for photochemical or photobiocatalytic reactions.

Directed Evolution

  • Full Name: Laboratory natural selection
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Iterative process of mutation and selection to engineer enzymes with improved or novel properties for biocatalytic applications.

Computational Enzyme Design

  • Full Name: In silico catalyst engineering
  • Type: Method
  • Notes: Use of computational tools to predict mutations that will improve enzyme performance or create novel enzymatic activities.

Process Intensification

  • Full Name: Reaction efficiency enhancement
  • Type: Strategy
  • Notes: Approach to drastically improve production efficiency through innovative equipment and processing techniques, often combining multiple operations.

Immobilized Cell Reactor

  • Full Name: Whole-cell biocatalyst reactor

Membrane Reactor

Biphasic System

  • Full Name: Two-phase reaction medium

Residence Time Distribution (RTD)

Structured Catalyst

Biofilm Reactor

Falling Film Microreactor

Switchable Catalysis

Common Abbreviations

HPLC

  • Full Name: High-Performance Liquid Chromatography
  • Type: Analytical technique
  • Notes: Method for separating, identifying, and quantifying components in a mixture, commonly used to monitor biocatalytic reactions.

UPLC

  • Full Name: Ultra-Performance Liquid Chromatography
  • Type: Analytical technique
  • Notes: Advanced form of HPLC using smaller particles and higher pressures for improved resolution and faster analysis.

GC

  • Full Name: Gas Chromatography
  • Type: Analytical technique
  • Notes: Separation technique for volatile compounds, often coupled with mass spectrometry for identification of biocatalysis products.

MS

  • Full Name: Mass Spectrometry
  • Type: Analytical technique
  • Notes: Method for identifying compounds based on their mass-to-charge ratio, frequently used in biocatalysis for product verification.

NMR

  • Full Name: Nuclear Magnetic Resonance
  • Type: Analytical technique
  • Notes: Spectroscopic method providing detailed structural information about reaction products and enzyme-substrate interactions.

MOPS

  • Full Name: 3-(N-morpholino)propanesulfonic acid
  • Type: Buffer
  • Notes: Good’s buffer commonly used for maintaining pH in the range of 6.5-7.9 in enzyme reactions.

HEPES

  • Full Name: 4-(2-hydroxyethyl)-1-piperazineethanesulfonic acid
  • Type: Buffer
  • Notes: Good’s buffer used for maintaining physiological pH (7.2-7.6) in enzyme reactions with minimal metal ion interactions.

TRIS

  • Full Name: Tris(hydroxymethyl)aminomethane
  • Type: Buffer
  • Notes: Common buffer for enzyme reactions in the pH range 7.0-9.0, though can interfere with some enzymes.

DTT

  • Full Name: Dithiothreitol
  • Type: Additive
  • Notes: Reducing agent used to prevent oxidation of cysteine residues in enzymes, maintaining catalytic activity.

EDTA

  • Full Name: Ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid
  • Type: Additive
  • Notes: Metal chelator used to sequester metal ions that might inhibit certain enzymes or cause oxidative damage.

IPTG

  • Full Name: Isopropyl β-D-1-thiogalactopyranoside
  • Type: Inducer
  • Notes: Molecular mimic of allolactose used to induce protein expression in recombinant enzyme production.

SDS-PAGE

  • Full Name: Sodium Dodecyl Sulfate-Polyacrylamide Gel Electrophoresis
  • Type: Analytical technique
  • Notes: Method for separating proteins based on molecular weight, used to verify enzyme purity.

SEC

  • Full Name: Size Exclusion Chromatography
  • Type: Purification technique
  • Notes: Chromatographic method separating proteins based on size, used in enzyme purification and analysis.

IEX

  • Full Name: Ion Exchange Chromatography
  • Type: Purification technique
  • Notes: Separation method based on protein surface charge, commonly used in enzyme purification.

IMAC

  • Full Name: Immobilized Metal Affinity Chromatography
  • Type: Purification technique
  • Notes: Purification technique utilizing metal ions (often Ni²⁺) to selectively bind histidine-tagged recombinant enzymes.

kcat

  • Full Name: Catalytic Constant
  • Type: Kinetic parameter
  • Notes: Rate constant representing the maximum number of substrate molecules converted to product per enzyme molecule per second.

KM

  • Full Name: Michaelis Constant
  • Type: Kinetic parameter
  • Notes: Substrate concentration at which the reaction rate is half of the maximum rate, indicating enzyme-substrate affinity.

kcat/KM

  • Full Name: Catalytic Efficiency
  • Type: Kinetic parameter
  • Notes: Ratio indicating enzyme efficiency, approaching the diffusion-controlled limit for catalytically perfect enzymes.

ee

  • Full Name: Enantiomeric excess
  • Type: Parameter
  • Notes: Measure of the optical purity of a chiral substance, expressed as a percentage.

de

  • Full Name: Diastereomeric excess
  • Type: Parameter
  • Notes: Measure of the purity of a mixture of diastereomers, expressed as a percentage.

Vmax

  • Full Name: Maximum Velocity
  • Type: Kinetic parameter
  • Notes: Maximum reaction rate achieved when enzyme is saturated with substrate.

ATP

  • Full Name: Adenosine Triphosphate
  • Type: Cofactor
  • Notes: Energy-rich molecule used in kinase reactions and other energy-requiring enzymatic processes.

GSH

  • Full Name: Glutathione (reduced form)
  • Type: Cofactor
  • Notes: Tripeptide thiol involved in many detoxification and redox enzymes as a nucleophile or redox buffer.

PLP

  • Full Name: Pyridoxal 5’-Phosphate
  • Type: Cofactor
  • Notes: Derivative of vitamin B6 essential for transamination, racemization, and other reactions involving amino acids.

SAM

  • Full Name: S-Adenosyl methionine
  • Type: Cofactor
  • Notes: Major methyl donor cofactor in enzymatic methylation reactions.

CLEA

  • Full Name: Cross-Linked Enzyme Aggregate
  • Type: Preparation
  • Notes: Carrier-free immobilized enzyme formed by precipitation followed by cross-linking.

CLEC

  • Full Name: Cross-Linked Enzyme Crystal
  • Type: Preparation
  • Notes: Immobilized enzyme preparation made by cross-linking crystallized enzyme molecules.

TTN

  • Full Name: Total Turnover Number
  • Type: Performance metric
  • Notes: Total number of moles of substrate converted per mole of enzyme used in an entire process.

DMF

  • Full Name: Dimethylformamide
  • Type: Solvent
  • Notes: Polar aprotic solvent sometimes used in biocatalysis to solubilize hydrophobic substrates.

DMSO

  • Full Name: Dimethyl sulfoxide
  • Type: Solvent
  • Notes: Polar aprotic solvent commonly used as co-solvent in biocatalytic reactions involving hydrophobic substrates.

PEG

  • Full Name: Polyethylene glycol
  • Type: Additive
  • Notes: Polymer used to modify enzyme surfaces, create two-phase systems, or as precipitant in enzyme purification.

MTBE

  • Full Name: Methyl tert-butyl ether
  • Type: Solvent
  • Notes: Organic solvent with moderate polarity used in biphasic biocatalytic reactions due to low enzyme deactivation.

LC-MS

  • Full Name: Liquid Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry
  • Type: Analytical technique
  • Notes: Combined separation and identification technique for analyzing reaction mixtures and determining product structures.

GC-MS

  • Full Name: Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry
  • Type: Analytical technique
  • Notes: Combined technique for volatile compound separation and identification, widely used for monitoring biocatalytic reactions.

FAD

  • Full Name: Flavin Adenine Dinucleotide
  • Type: Cofactor
  • Notes: Redox cofactor involved in numerous oxidoreductase reactions, exists in oxidized (FAD) and reduced (FADH₂) forms.

FMN

  • Full Name: Flavin Mononucleotide
  • Type: Cofactor
  • Notes: Derivative of riboflavin (vitamin B2) that functions as a cofactor in various redox enzymes.

DERA

  • Full Name: 2-Deoxyribose-5-phosphate Aldolase
  • Type: Enzyme
  • Notes: Biocatalyst used for stereoselective aldol reactions, popular in industrial aldol condensations.

His-tag

  • Full Name: Polyhistidine affinity tag
  • Type: Protein modification
  • Notes: Series of histidine residues added to recombinant enzymes to facilitate purification by metal affinity chromatography.

DOE

  • Full Name: Design of Experiments
  • Type: Methodology
  • Notes: Statistical approach to efficiently optimize reaction parameters in biocatalytic processes.

QbD

  • Full Name: Quality by Design
  • Type: Framework
  • Notes: Systematic approach to develop biocatalytic processes with predefined quality attributes rather than testing quality after production.

GMP

  • Full Name: Good Manufacturing Practice
  • Type: Standards
  • Notes: Regulations for quality assurance in production processes, especially important for biocatalytic synthesis of pharmaceuticals.

TLC

  • Full Name: Thin Layer Chromatography
  • Type: Analytical technique
  • Notes: Simple chromatographic method used for quick monitoring of biocatalytic reactions and product formation.
