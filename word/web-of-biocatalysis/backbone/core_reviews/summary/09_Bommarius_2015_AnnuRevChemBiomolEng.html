Biocatalysis: A Status Report (Bommarius, Annu. Rev. Chem. Biomol. Eng. 2015)

Citation & scope
Field overview focused on three historic bottlenecks—designability, stability, and limited off-the-shelf enzyme panels—and on practical progress in protein engineering, quantitative stability/TTN modeling, and discovery/creation of new biocatalysts.

Thesis / what’s new
Positions a “third wave” of data-driven/semirational engineering (restricted libraries, CASTing, ISM) plus quantitative process stability models (TTN/kd,obs) as the enablers turning bespoke enzymes into a broader, reliable toolbox; showcases both novel native functions and engineered/de-novo reactivities entering synthesis.

Higher-order topic map (authors’ framing)

  1. Strengths vs weaknesses — stellar selectivity and mild conditions vs (i) slow/serendipitous design, (ii) limited operational stability, (iii) too few ready enzymes.

  2. Protein-engineering playbook — restricted libraries, CASTing, ISM to shrink libraries yet raise hit rates; SCHEMA; consensus design; B-FIT (stabilize mobile residues); FRESCO (computational library → screen).

  3. Stability as a process metric — rigorous TTN = kcat,obs/kd,obs under Lumry–Eyring deactivation; distinguish thermodynamic Tm from kinetic (process) stability; report STY, and for synthesis favor v_avg and K_M,eff over just kcat or kcat/KM.

  4. Multi-stress robustness — correlations across heat/solvent/salt/interface stresses; examples where thermostabilization co-improves solvent tolerance.

  5. Novel native functions — inverting alkylsulfatases (no chemical analogue), alkene “ozonidase” oxidations, BBE-mediated oxidative Pictet–Spengler chemistry.

  6. Engineered & de-novo reactivity — MAO-N evolution for deracemizations; AmDHs from AADHs (two-mutation switch); P450→P411 platforms for carbene/nitrene transfer (cyclopropanation, C–H amination, N–H insertion); computational Kemp eliminases.

  7. Applications — pharma case studies (e.g., atorvastatin side-chain via ADH/KRED/HHDH routes) underline process-level metrics and cofactor/DSP tactics.

Key exemplars & metrics

  • Engineering tools: CASTing/ISM schematics; restricted codons cut screens by orders of magnitude at ≥95% coverage.

  • Stability leaps: xylanase Tm 61→96 °C; consensus-engineered GDH 10⁶× half-life increase; LEH via FRESCO +35 °C Tm, ~250× kd improvement.

  • Cross-stress wins: thermostable lipase A variants also more solvent-tolerant; thermophilic TOYE resists heat/solvent/denaturants better than mesophilic OYEs.

  • Operational modeling: TTN formula; v_avg ≈ vmax/(1 + K_M,eff/[S]0) at 99% conversion; define Ef to compare variants at high [S].

  • New native reactions: alkylsulfatases (E up to >200 with medium tuning); alkene oxidase cleaving styrenes to carbonyls with high chemoselectivity; BBE deracemization/cyclizations in biphasic media, >97% ee.

  • Engineered functions: MAO-N lineage (D-series) expands from primary → tertiary amines; AmDH from LeuDH/PheDH (2-site switch + CASTing); P411 variants: cyclopropanation (up to 97% ee), C–H amination (TTN ~383, 73% ee).

  • Pharma: L. brevis ADH reduction for Lipitor side-chain (≥99.5% ee; optimized NADP⁺ TTN); alternative KRED/HHDH routes (Codexis).

Limits / gaps (authors’ view)
Sparse public STY/throughput data; overreliance on kcat/KM for synthesis conditions; enzyme catalogs still thin for many transformations; design still needs assays + structure; standardization across reporting remains needed.

Outlook
Marry semirational design with quantitative stability targets and assayable new functions; expect further convergence of stabilization + scope expansion (native and abiological) and more case-driven industrial adoption.

Tags

Biocatalysis Field Topics — From Bommarius 2015 (“Biocatalysis: A Status Report”)

Document ID: Bommarius_2015_AnnualRevChemBioEng

What this review sets out to do. It frames three core bottlenecks—limited designability, stability ceilings, and a shortage of ready-to-use enzymes—then surveys how new protein-engineering tools, quantitative stability models, and genuinely new-to-biology enzymes are closing those gaps.

Core topics extracted

  1. Process-relevant performance metrics (not just kcat/KM). Introduces the efficiency function (Ef) and catalytic effectiveness (_v_avg at high conversion) as better predictors under industrial substrate loads.

  2. Total Turnover Number (TTN) as lifetime output. Links TTN to unfolding/deactivation via Lumry–Eyring kinetics; gives simple relations between kcat, kd,obs and TTN.

  3. Space–time yield thinking. Ties productivity to solubility constraints ([S]max, [P]max) and residence time—grounding bench metrics in reactor reality.

  4. Restricted libraries for directed evolution. Shows why restricted saturation and coverage planning beat naïve random mutagenesis; quantifies transformants needed.

  5. CASTing (Combinatorial Active-Site Saturation Testing). Targeted multi-site saturation guided by structure/mechanism; practical coverage (2–4 positions).

  6. Iterative Saturation Mutagenesis (ISM). Multi-round CASTing to capture epistasis and synergy; pathway-independent properties for final variants.

  7. Stability modeling that predicts output. Quantitative links between kinetic/thermodynamic stability and process lifetime; emphasizes kd,obs over Tm alone.

  8. Cross-protection across stresses. Improvements to heat stability often bring solvent/interfacial robustness (examples: lipase A via B-FIT; thermophilic OYEs).

  9. Computational stabilization pipelines (e.g., FRESCO). Practical flow from structure → in silico ΔΔG filters → MD screens → combine stabilizing mutations.

  10. Consensus & recombination for stability. Consensus GDHs tolerate salts/solvents; SCHEMA-recombined cellulases validate additivity and single hot-spot fixes.

  11. Medium & interface engineering. Stabilization against bubbles, shear, and cavitation (e.g., FDH Cys→Ser/Ala) and biphasic/solvent choices that boost selectivity.

  12. MAO-N evolution for deracemization of (secondary/tertiary) amines. Stepwise pocket enlargement across D-series variants; crystal-structure rationale.

  13. Amine dehydrogenases (AmDHs). Evolved ADHs → AmDHs enabling reductive amination as a single-enzyme transform (process-relevant chiral amines).

  14. Cytochrome P450s with new reactivity. Engineered P450BM3 variants perform abiological carbene/nitrene transfer-like chemistry and control cis/trans + e.e. at scale-relevant yields.

  15. Alkene “ozonidases.” Discovery of a Mn³⁺ enzyme from Trametes hirsuta that cleaves alkenes to carbonyls with O₂—mechanistically distinct from (mono)dioxygenases.

  16. Bicovalent-flavin enzymes (BBE) for Pictet–Spengler chemistry. Solvent-compatible oxidative cyclizations/deracemizations broaden heterocycle synthesis options.

  17. Enantiocomplementary sulfatases. Inverting/retaining sulfatases give anti-Kazlauskas access to (S)-alcohols; simple medium tweaks jump E from ~4 to >200.

  18. De novo designed enzymes (Kemp eliminases). KE70/KE07/KE59 show computation → evolution pipelines; proficiency rises 10⁶–10⁸-fold with stability rescue.

  19. Metric awareness for variant ranking. Warns that “vmax/KM-is-fine” thinking can mislead at high conversion due to product inhibition; advocates _v_avg.

  20. Industrial anchoring. Connects metrics to case landscapes (statin side-chains, etc.) and emphasizes confidentiality barriers around productivity datasets.

Why this review is distinctive

  • Metrics that matter. It centers Ef, _v_avg (to 99% conversion), TTN and space–time yield—tools chem/bioprocess folks can actually use to triage variants and size reactors.

  • From “stability is Tm” to “stability is output.” Provides model-based, stress-aware stabilization strategies and shows cross-stress correlations (T₅₀ vs C₅₀).

  • New enzymes, not just better ones. Highlights genuinely novel reactivity (ozonidases, BBE) and computational de novo catalysts, expanding the synthetic playbook.

How it plugs into your backbone

  • Bridges Reetz’s evolutionary toolkit and Arnold’s “new chemistry” with process-valid performance metrics and stability engineering that translate to plant conditions.

  • Complements earlier industry reviews by giving concrete, quantitative screens/targets for variant selection (Ef/_v_avg/TTN) rather than only conversion/yield narratives.
