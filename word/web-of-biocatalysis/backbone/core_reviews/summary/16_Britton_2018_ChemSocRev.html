16_Britton_2018_ChemSocRev

Continuous flow biocatalysis (Britton, Majumdar, Weiss, Chem. Soc. Rev. 2018)

Citation & scope
Comprehensive review of equipment, immobilization modes, reactor designs, and applications for enzymes/whole cells in continuous flow; includes PAT, in-line separations, and multi-step chemoenzymatic sequences.

Thesis / what’s new
Pairing enzymes with flow hardware (pumps + BPRs + packed beds/microreactors/tube-in-tube) delivers higher STY, tighter control, easier reuse, and in-line workup; lipases dominate but scope spans oxidations, reductions, transaminations, phosphorylations, polymerizations, and cascades.

Higher-order topic map (authors’ framing)

  1. Flow hardware & basics — pumps (semi-continuous vs continuous), back-pressure regulators to open high-T windows, reactor types: coils, chips, packed beds, cartridges, tube-in-tube for gases.

  2. Biocatalyst formats — whole cell vs purified enzyme: trade-offs in cofactor supply, mass transfer, specificity, stability.

  3. Immobilization strategies — adsorption, covalent (epoxides, GA, silica/chitosan), affinity (His-tag/Ni-NTA), entrapment (alginate, mesoporous silica, nanowires). Design asks: area, hydrophilicity, rigidity, regeneration, cost.

  4. Unit operations in flow — in-line analytics (UV), catch-and-release cartridges, in-line separators (Zaiput), biphasic and IL media, recycle loops.

  5. Cofactor management — GDH/FDH/G6PDH recycling, IPA “borrowing” for KREDs, co-immobilized enzyme+NADP(H) for self-sufficiency.

  6. Applications — whole-cell reductions/resolutions, lipases (esterification, epoxidation via in situ peracids, polymerizations), HNL cyanohydrins, transaminations, phosphorylations, P450/oxygenases with gas transfer, wastewater lipid hydrolysis.

  7. Cascades & spatial control — multi-step chemo-bio sequences, magnetic-bead microreactors, thin-film IMAC for rapid purify-&-immobilize and patterned zones.

Key exemplars & metrics

  • Whole-cell nitrile hydratase, dual packed beds at 4 °C: STY 93.2 g·L⁻¹·h⁻¹, stable 240 h (but at 30 °C lost >60% in 40 h).

  • Trehalosyl-maltodextrin (immobilized E. coli): 106.5 mg·mL⁻¹·h⁻¹ at 30% substrate; immobilization efficiency vs activity trade-off quantified.

  • scCO₂ whole-cell reduction of cyclohexanone: ~2× batch rate (0.24 vs 0.13 mmol·min⁻¹).

  • Lactose hydrolysis (immobilized K. lactis): ~40% at 125 min τ; CSTR ~quantitative at 600 min; cells stable 7 weeks at 4 °C.

  • (2R,5R)-hexanediol from 2,5-hexanedione (L. kefiri): 64 g·L⁻¹·d⁻¹, >99% ee, 19× better than batch; 6-day run kept 68% activity.

  • (S)-1-phenylethanol (R. glutinis): 75% yield; 30-day operation.

  • Biodiesel (immob. A. niger): 90% FAME at 250 mL·min⁻¹; higher flow improved mixing/yield.

  • Flurbiprofen resolution (dry mycelium): 2.64 mL·min⁻¹·g⁻¹, 90% ee, τ = 6.5 min (Vapourtec).

  • KRED/Novozym 435: alkyl esters ~97–100% conv (1 mL·min⁻¹); epoxidations 97–99% with BPR 100 psig (70 °C).

  • Ring-opening polymerization in microchannels: faster, higher Mw, <1% leaching.

  • Two-enzyme + chemical cyanohydrin sequence (Ley): HCN from cyanoformate 97% in 17.5 min (5 bar); HNL step 99% conv, 95% ee; acylation gives 75–99% yield.

  • Wastewater edible-oil hydrolysis (lipase on rice-husk carbon): up to 99% removal (τ = 4 h).

  • CAPE synthesis in IL with Novozym 435: 93% yield, τ = 2.5 h, stable 9 days.

  • Two-phase microfluidic KRED (PETN reductase) with in-line UV monitoring: higher yields than batch.

  • Acid phosphatase phosphorylation (covalent on PMA beads): 32 g, 78% isolated over 12 days; fed-batch comparable.

  • Transketolase multi-input reactor: 4.5× higher output concentration, 5× throughput (overcomes substrate inhibition).

  • Tube-in-tube O₂ hydroxylation: 17.7 g·L⁻¹·h⁻¹ STY.

  • o-Transaminase whole-cell packed bed: >99% ee amines; stable 5 days; silica catch-and-release workup.

  • Magnetic-bead microreactor cascade (ordered enzymes): >95% conv, >99:1 d.r. by spatial ordering.

  • Thin-film IMAC: 10-min purify+immobilize from lysate; 5-day run, 0.34% leaching; zone-wise kcat/KM balancing.

  • KRED + NADP(H) co-immobilized (IPA recycling): 80% yield; STY 104 g·L⁻¹·d⁻¹; 120 h stable.

  • Immobilized HEw-TA (Paradisi) with in-line separation: aldehydes → amines in 2 min vs 24 h batch; 50–99% isolated yields.

Limits / gaps (authors’ view)
Mass-transfer barriers (whole cells, viscous feeds), enzyme leaching/denaturation, IL cost/greenness, accurate batch vs flow comparisons often lacking, need for standardized reporting (flow rate, reactor volume/ID, τ, BPR, T).

Outlook
Growth in packed-bed and microfluidic cascades, gas-liquid tube-in-tube oxygenations, cofactor self-sufficiency, and in-line purification; off-the-shelf systems (cartridges, separators, PAT) will democratize flow biocatalysis and speed API and functional materials manufacture.
