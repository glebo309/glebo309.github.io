Biocatalysis in Ionic Liquids (van Rantwijk & Sheldon, Chem. Rev. 2007)

Citation & scope
Foundational review of enzyme and whole-cell catalysis in ionic liquids (ILs): solvent properties, enzyme compatibility (aqueous vs nearly anhydrous ILs), reaction classes (lipases/esterases, proteases, glycosidases, redox enzymes, HNLs), and process/DS options (recycling, evaporation, biphasic scCO₂, IL membranes).

Thesis / what’s new
ILs offer non-volatile, tunable media that often match/exceed classic organic solvents for activity, stability, and (enantio)selectivity; no single “best IL” exists—medium must be tuned to enzyme + substrate. Emerging process tricks (e.g., scCO₂/IL biphasic, product evaporation, IL membranes) hint at greener, recyclable biocatalysis.

Higher-order topic map (authors’ framing)

  1. IL fundamentals & enzyme compatibility — purity, water activity, (ill-defined) polarity; compatibility not predictable from simple scales; impurities (e.g., halide/HF) can deactivate enzymes.

  2. Aqueous IL mixtures — many enzymes tolerate 30–70% IL; effects aren’t cleanly explained by Hofmeister series; pH shifts and a_w matter.

  3. Nearly anhydrous ILs — hydrolases (lipases esp.) work well; activity/solubility is anion-dependent; dissolution can mean denaturation unless using special ILs that preserve activity.

  4. Reaction classes — Lipases/esterases (resolutions, perhydrolysis, aminolysis), proteases (peptide/amide chemistry, resolutions), glycosidases (carbohydrate derivatization), redox enzymes, HNL lyases.

  5. Whole cells in IL systems — certain hydrophobic ILs preserve membrane integrity better than typical organic solvents.

  6. Process intensification — catalyst recycling by phase-split with ethers; product/water evaporation (ILs have near-zero vapor pressure); IL–scCO₂ biphasic for continuous extraction; IL membranes for facilitated transport/resolution.

Key exemplars & metrics

  • scCO₂/IL biphasic lipase: Novozym 435 retained in IL, substrates/products in scCO₂; rate ~8× higher than pure scCO₂, high operational stability; with in-situ racemization gave ~80% yield and >97% ee (IL-dependent).

  • Kinetic resolutions in ILs: For arylalkanols (e.g., 21, 23, 26–28), E often improved vs TBME/toluene; for methyl mandelate (24) E 10→>250 depending on immobilization carrier + IL.

  • Medium sensitivity/impurities: Opposite outcomes for 1-phenylethanol resolution across labs traced to IL impurities (e.g., halide/HF); esterases far less tolerant than lipases.

  • Non-natural lipase chemistry: Aminolysis to octanamide in [BMIm][BF₄] at 40–70% of tert-BuOH rate; perhydrolysis epoxidation in IL slightly slower than ACN.

  • Carbohydrate & flavonoid acylations: IL choice flips rate/selectivity; selective acylation of naringin and rutin showed enzyme/IL-dependent activity order changes.

  • Downstream tricks: Swap vinyl → methyl esters and evaporate methanol to drive transesterifications to completion without acetaldehyde inhibition; easy ether extraction leaves enzyme in IL for reuse.

  • IL membranes: Supported IL membranes enabled ibuprofen resolutions; [BMIm][NTf₂] gave higher permeate fluxes than dodecane.

Limits / gaps (authors’ view)

  • Predictability gap: no robust theory to predict enzyme–IL compatibility; polarity/logP/Hofmeister often fail.

  • Purity & toxicity: chloride/HF impurities deactivate enzymes; classic imidazolium ILs raise eco-toxicity/biodegradability concerns—need greener ILs.

  • Enzyme class limits: many esterases/proteases require some water; penicillin G acylase needs high a_w (~0.8) even in ILs.

Outlook (authors)
Expect cheaper, biocompatible ILs; wider adoption where ILs enable highly polar substrates or innovative DS (evaporation, scCO₂ extraction, membranes). Biggest “green” wins likely from process design (IL+scCO₂, recycling, multicatalyst cascades), not just solvent swaps.
