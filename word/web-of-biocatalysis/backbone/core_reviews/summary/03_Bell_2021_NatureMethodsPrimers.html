Biocatalysis (Primer) (Bell et al., Nat. Rev. Methods Primers 2021)

Citation & scope
A methods-primer that walks the end-to-end pipeline: route design (manual & CASP/RetroBioCat), biocatalyst sourcing (commercial kits, biosynthetic enzymes, metagenomes, in-silico mining), optimization (directed evolution, computation, ncAAs), cascade build (design–build–optimize), and bioprocess metrics/implementation.

Thesis / what’s new
Biocatalysis is mainstream because discovery (databases, gene synthesis), laboratory evolution, and computational design now deliver tailored enzymes fast; standardized data/ontologies and reporting are emerging, while bio-retrosynthesis and CASP tools make de-novo cascades practical (e.g., islatravir).

Higher-order topic map (authors’ framing)

  1. Route design & tools — Manual + RetroBioCat for bio-retrosynthesis; iterate reaction design ⇄ enzyme choice ⇄ bioprocess.

  2. Sources of biocatalysts — (a) Commercial libraries (KREDs, IREDs, TAs, BVMOs, MAO-N, lyases), (b) biosynthetic enzymes (e.g., Fe(II)/α-KG hydroxylases/halogenases), (c) metagenomic & in-silico mining; rapid gene synthesis makes any reported sequence accessible.

  3. Directed evolution — Focused libraries (CAST/ISM, reduced codons) and high-throughput screens (plates, colony colorimetry, FADS/AADS droplets, displays, continuous evolution) are the workhorse.

  4. Computational design — Theozyme→Rosetta, multistate/MD-guided designs, tunnel analysis (CAVER), FRESCO stabilization; dynamics/allostery and distal mutations matter.

  5. Genetic code expansion (ncAAs) — Install new functional groups; evolve aaRS; demonstrated de-novo hydrolase OE1→OE1.3 with >9,000-fold rate boost vs free Me-His.

  6. Cascade development — Design–Build–Optimize loop; linear/orthogonal/cyclic topologies; in-vitro, in-vivo, or hybrid; cofactor, compatibility, immobilization, ISR considered up-front.

  7. What makes a good industrial biocatalyst — High activity at high loads, solvent/pH/temperature tolerance, operational stability (TTN), immobilizability, and strong green/economic metrics (PMI, E-factor, productivity).

  8. Standards & infrastructure — Data/metadata, STRENDA, SiLA/AnIML, enzyme repositories; Revenant for ancestral proteins; IP/FTO awareness.

Key exemplars & metrics

  • Acrylamide (NHase): 50–100 g·L⁻¹·h⁻¹ productivities at 300–500 g·L⁻¹ titers; a flagship whole-cell process.

  • Sitagliptin (TA): substrate walking + evolution → 200 g·L⁻¹ load, 45 °C, 50% DMSO, >99.9% ee; beats Rh catalysis on cost/green metrics.

  • Islatravir: 9-enzyme cascade (5 evolved) with ATP/phosphate shuttling; high overall yield without workups.

  • IREDs: pilot-scale 13 g·L⁻¹·h⁻¹; engineered IRED for GSK2879552 shows 38,000× turnover gain vs WT.

  • (–)-Ambrox (SHC): whole-cell cyclization, ~12 g·L⁻¹·h⁻¹ after DoE optimization.

Limits / gaps (authors’ view)
ML/design still limited by data quality/metadata; screening modality biases (e.g., fluorescence only in droplets); scale logistics for cofactors; broad, oxidative C–C coupling remains challenging; need consistent reporting of process-relevant metrics.

Outlook
Expect tighter automation + standards, bigger public enzyme libraries, expanded ncAA and de-novo platforms, and increasingly routine designer cascades bridging biocatalysis and biotransformations for complex targets.

Tags
Route design & RetroBioCat; commercial & biosynthetic enzyme sources; metagenomics/in-silico; directed evolution (FADS/AADS/continuous); computation (Rosetta/FRESCO/CAVER); ncAAs; cascades (DBO loop); industrial metrics (PMI/E-factor/STY); standards (STRENDA, SiLA/AnIML).

Biocatalysis Field Topics - From Bell et al. Nature Methods Primers 2021

Document ID: Bell_2021_NatureMethodsPrimers

Core Topics Extracted:

1. Commercial Enzyme Sources & Libraries

  • Definition: Ready-made enzyme collections from vendors for direct screening
  • Key aspects: KRED libraries, IRED libraries, transaminase collections, oxidoreductase panels
  • Applications: Rapid biocatalyst identification, substrate-specific screening

2. Natural Product Biosynthetic Enzymes

  • Definition: Enzymes from specialized metabolite pathways repurposed for synthesis
  • Key aspects: Fe(II)/2-oxoglutarate dependent enzymes, halogenases, methyltransferases
  • Applications: C-H functionalization, stereoselective transformations

3. Metagenomic & In Silico Enzyme Mining

  • Definition: Discovery of new biocatalysts from environmental DNA and sequence databases
  • Key aspects: Function-based screening, sequence-based screening, degenerate primers
  • Applications: Novel enzyme discovery, extreme environment adaptation

4. High-Throughput Screening Platforms

  • Definition: Automated systems for rapid evaluation of enzyme variants
  • Key aspects: Microfluidics, droplet sorting, fluorescence-activated screening
  • Applications: Directed evolution campaigns, library screening

5. DNA Library Generation Strategies

  • Definition: Methods for creating genetic diversity in protein engineering
  • Key aspects: Error-prone PCR, saturation mutagenesis, DNA shuffling, CAST
  • Applications: Focused evolution, combinatorial optimization

6. Computational Enzyme Design

  • Definition: Computer-aided approaches for rational enzyme engineering
  • Key aspects: Rosetta design, molecular dynamics, QM/MM calculations, CASCO
  • Applications: Active site redesign, stability enhancement

7. Machine Learning in Protein Engineering

  • Definition: AI-guided approaches for predicting beneficial mutations
  • Key aspects: Sequence-function relationships, predictive modeling, smart libraries
  • Applications: Reducing screening burden, accelerating optimization

8. Non-Canonical Amino Acid Incorporation

  • Definition: Expanding the chemical alphabet of proteins beyond 20 natural amino acids
  • Key aspects: Orthogonal tRNA synthetases, genetic code expansion, novel chemistries
  • Applications: New catalytic mechanisms, enhanced stability

9. Enzyme Cascade Design & Development

  • Definition: Multi-step biocatalytic processes in single reaction vessels
  • Key aspects: Linear cascades, orthogonal cascades, cyclic cascades, retrobiosynthesis
  • Applications: Complex molecule synthesis, telescoped processes

10. Computer-Aided Synthesis Planning (CASP)

  • Definition: Automated tools for designing biocatalytic routes
  • Key aspects: RetroBioCat, pathway prediction, enzyme database integration
  • Applications: Route design, cascade planning

11. Bioprocess Development & Scale-Up

  • Definition: Translation from lab-scale to industrial manufacturing
  • Key aspects: Fed-batch strategies, ISPR, continuous processing, immobilization
  • Applications: Industrial implementation, cost optimization

12. Industrial Process Metrics

  • Definition: Quantitative measures for evaluating biocatalytic processes
  • Key aspects: Productivity (g/L/h), E-factor, atom economy, process mass intensity
  • Applications: Sustainability assessment, process comparison

13. Enzyme Immobilization Technologies

  • Definition: Methods for binding enzymes to solid supports for reuse
  • Key aspects: CLEAs, carrier-bound enzymes, flow systems, operational stability
  • Applications: Continuous processing, enzyme recovery

14. Ultra-High-Throughput Methods

  • Definition: Screening technologies capable of >10^6 variants
  • Key aspects: Droplet microfluidics, FACS, continuous evolution, selection-based approaches
  • Applications: Massive library screening, rare variant discovery

15. Biocatalyst Databases & Data Management

  • Definition: Repositories for storing and accessing enzyme data
  • Key aspects: Substrate specificity, reaction conditions, BioCatNet, PrenDB
  • Applications: Enzyme selection, knowledge sharing

16. Cell-Free Protein Expression

  • Definition: In vitro systems for rapid enzyme production without cells
  • Key aspects: Reduced cycle times, direct enzyme access, high-throughput compatible
  • Applications: Accelerated development, prototyping

17. Continuous Flow Biocatalysis

  • Definition: Steady-state enzymatic processes in flow reactors
  • Key aspects: Immobilized enzymes, residence time control, process intensification
  • Applications: Industrial manufacturing, multi-step synthesis

18. Biocatalytic Late-Stage Functionalization

  • Definition: Enzyme-catalyzed modifications of complex molecules
  • Key aspects: C-H activation, selective oxidation, site-specific modification
  • Applications: Pharmaceutical diversification, compound libraries

19. Green Chemistry Metrics in Biocatalysis

  • Definition: Environmental impact assessment of enzymatic processes
  • Key aspects: Carbon footprint, waste reduction, renewable feedstocks
  • Applications: Sustainability evaluation, process optimization

20. Reproducibility Standards (STRENDA)

  • Definition: Guidelines for standardized reporting of enzyme data
  • Key aspects: Experimental conditions, kinetic parameters, data validation
  • Applications: Data quality, cross-study comparisons

Backbone Categories

  1. Route & Reaction Design — bio-retrosynthesis/CASP tools (e.g., RetroBioCat), manual planning.

  2. Enzyme Sourcing & Discovery — commercial kits, natural-product biosynthesis enzymes, metagenomic & in-silico mining.

  3. Protein Engineering (Directed Evolution) — focused libraries, ISM/CASTing, reduced codons, recombination.

  4. Computational Design — Rosetta/QM/MM/EVB, conformational dynamics, multistate design, FRESCO/CASCO.

  5. Genetic Code Expansion (ncAA) — orthogonal aaRS/tRNA, mechanistic probes, new catalytic motifs.

  6. Screening & Selection Platforms — plate/LC assays, colony colorimetric, microfluidics/FADS, phage display, continuous evolution.

  7. Cascades & Pathway Assembly — design–build–optimize cycles; in vitro / in vivo / hybrid implementations.

  8. Cofactors & Compatibility — cofactor needs/regen, solvent and pH tolerance, high-load operation considerations.

  9. Immobilization & Operational Stability — reuse, lifetime, flow readiness, ISPR interfaces.
