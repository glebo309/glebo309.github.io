Power of Biocatalysis for Organic Synthesis (Winkler, Schrittwieser, Kroutil, ACS Cent. Sci. 2021)

Citation & scope
Tutorial/overview arguing that enzymes are now a mainstream toolbox for organic synthesis, detailing how active-site 3D confinement + dynamics give high chemo/regio/enantioselectivity; tours core reaction classes, new-to-nature reactivity, and cascades (industrial case studies included).

Thesis / what’s new
Emphasizes enzymes as “high-tech catalysts”: precise substrate positioning (≥3 interactions), gated tunnels, conformational dynamics/allostery, and synergistic residues across shells; stability can be engineered (ancestral reconstruction, salt bridges, disulfides, glycosylation). Modern routes mix single-step biotransformations with multi-enzyme cascades, and evolution/computation keep expanding scope into abiological reactions.

Higher-order topic map (authors’ framing)

  1. How enzymes achieve selectivity — binding/activation/TS stabilization/product release; tunnel gating & backbone motion; trade-off between scope and selectivity; libraries + evolution to tune both.

  2. Established transformations — hydrolases (kinetic/DKR of esters/amides), ADHs (ketone/aldehyde → alcohol), TAs / IREDs / RedAms / AmDHs (amines), OYEs, CARs, P450s/monooxygenases, peroxygenases, halogenases; compact “menu” in Table 2.

  3. New-to-nature catalysis — reaction promiscuity (e.g., 4-oxalocrotonate tautomerase organocatalyst-like chemistry), photo-biocatalysis with flavin EREDs (radical pathways), engineered P450→P411 for carbene/nitrene transfer (C–C, C–H amination), and artificial metalloenzymes (streptavidin–biotin “Suzukiase”).

  4. Cascades — enzymes’ operational compatibility enables one-pot, equilibrium-driven sequences; showpieces include atorvastatin intermediate and islatravir.

  5. Process & pedagogy — productivity/stability still limiters; call for bigger, public enzyme libraries, repositories, and ML-assisted design; integrate biocatalysis into chem curricula.

Key exemplars & metrics

  • Pregabalin route: lipase kinetic resolution → E-factor 86 → 17 vs 1st-gen process (waste ↓5×).

  • Atorvastatin intermediate (ADH reduction + HHDH): 95% isolated yield, >99.9% ee, STY 480 g·L⁻¹·d⁻¹ after enzyme evolution/cofactor engineering.

  • Sitagliptin TA: “substrate walking” over 11 rounds, 27 mutations → 275 g·L⁻¹ substrate, 88% isolated yield, >99.95% ee; productivity +56%, waste −19% vs Rh/Josiphos route.

  • Islatravir cascade: 9 enzymes (5 evolved), no workups, 51% overall yield; design uses oxidation → phosphorylation → aldol assembly + phosphate shuttling/ATP recycling to bias equilibria.

  • New bonds: engineered P450/P411 for cyclopropanation and C–H amination (high ee, nitrene transfer); Rma cyt c and Suzukiase illustrate heme and hybrid scaffolds enabling abiological steps.

Limits / gaps (authors’ view)
Catalyst stability/lifetime dictates STY; development time/costs still high; broader C–C coupling (esp. oxidative) remains a frontier; predictive design of dynamics/distal mutations and scalable SAM/ATP cofactor logistics are pain points.

Outlook
Expect larger ready-to-use enzyme libraries, repository distribution, and increasing ML-guided design; more tight, active-site-controlled transformations (including oxidative C–C) and hybrid catalysts will further compress routes; biocatalysis education is pivotal for adoption.

Tags
Active-site confinement & dynamics; hydrolases/ADHs/TAs/IREDs/RedAms/AmDHs; CARs/OYEs; P450/peroxygenase/halogenase; photo-biocatalysis; P411 carbene/nitrene; artificial metalloenzymes (Suzukiase); cascades (atorvastatin, islatravir); E-factor/STY; libraries & repositories; ML & education.

Core Topics Extracted:

1. Enzyme Structure-Function Relationships

  • Definition: Understanding how 3D protein architecture drives catalytic performance
  • Key aspects: Active site design, tunnel networks, conformational dynamics, allosteric regulation
  • Applications: Rational enzyme engineering, mechanism elucidation

2. Synergistic Catalysis in Enzymes

  • Definition: Multiple catalytic residues working cooperatively within active sites
  • Key aspects: Multi-point substrate binding, residue cooperation, transition state stabilization
  • Applications: Design principles for artificial enzymes, understanding natural efficiency

3. Enzyme Engineering via Directed Evolution

  • Definition: Laboratory evolution techniques to optimize enzyme properties
  • Key aspects: Substrate walking, library generation, high-throughput screening
  • Applications: Industrial catalyst development, novel activity introduction

4. Kinetic Resolution Processes

  • Definition: Enantioselective transformation of racemic mixtures
  • Key aspects: Dynamic kinetic resolution, lipase-catalyzed reactions, stereoselectivity enhancement
  • Applications: Pharmaceutical intermediate synthesis, chiral compound production

5. Cofactor-Dependent Biocatalysis

  • Definition: Enzymatic processes requiring non-protein helpers for activity
  • Key aspects: NAD(P)H recycling, ATP-dependent reactions, metal cofactors
  • Applications: Reduction reactions, energy-coupled transformations

6. Alcohol Dehydrogenase Systems

  • Definition: Enzymes catalyzing stereoselective carbonyl reduction
  • Key aspects: Prelog’s rule, stereocomplementary enzymes, cofactor recycling
  • Applications: Chiral alcohol synthesis, pharmaceutical building blocks

7. Transaminase Technology

  • Definition: PLP-dependent enzymes for stereoselective amine synthesis
  • Key aspects: Amine donor strategies, equilibrium shifting, substrate scope expansion
  • Applications: Pharmaceutical APIs, chiral amine libraries

8. Imine Reductase & Reductive Aminase Systems

  • Definition: NADPH-dependent enzymes for direct reductive amination
  • Key aspects: Stereocomplementary variants, substrate tolerance, equilibrium control
  • Applications: One-step amine synthesis, avoiding pre-formed imines

9. Carboxylic Acid Reductase Technology

  • Definition: ATP/NADPH-dependent reduction of carboxylic acids to aldehydes
  • Key aspects: Thioester intermediates, adenylation activity, amide formation capability
  • Applications: Aldehyde synthesis, biocatalytic amidation

10. Ene-Reductase Applications

  • Definition: Flavin-dependent enzymes for stereoselective alkene reduction
  • Key aspects: Activated alkene requirement, stereoselectivity, photobiocatalytic potential
  • Applications: Pharmaceutical synthesis, novel photochemical reactions

11. Cytochrome P450 Engineering

  • Definition: Heme-dependent monooxygenases for selective oxidations
  • Key aspects: C-H activation, carbene/nitrene chemistry, P411 variants
  • Applications: Late-stage functionalization, new-to-nature reactions

12. Promiscuous Enzyme Activities

  • Definition: Non-natural reactions catalyzed by wild-type enzymes
  • Key aspects: Catalytic promiscuity, substrate promiscuity, reaction scope expansion
  • Applications: Novel transformation discovery, evolutionary starting points

13. Photobiocatalysis

  • Definition: Light-activated enzymatic transformations using photoexcited cofactors
  • Key aspects: Flavin photoexcitation, radical chemistry, C-C bond formation
  • Applications: Radical cyclizations, stereoselective photochemistry

14. Artificial Metalloenzymes

  • Definition: Hybrid catalysts combining proteins with abiotic metal centers
  • Key aspects: Biotin-streptavidin technology, chiral environment creation, metal coordination
  • Applications: Asymmetric cross-coupling, novel metal-catalyzed reactions

15. 4-Oxalocrotonate Tautomerase Engineering

  • Definition: Short peptide enzyme repurposed for organocatalytic transformations
  • Key aspects: Proline-based catalysis, conjugate additions, epoxidations
  • Applications: Asymmetric Michael additions, aldol reactions

16. Biocatalytic Cascade Design

  • Definition: Multi-step enzymatic processes in single reaction vessels
  • Key aspects: Reaction compatibility, equilibrium manipulation, intermediate stability
  • Applications: Complex molecule synthesis, waste reduction

17. Enzyme Stability Engineering

  • Definition: Methods to enhance biocatalyst robustness under process conditions
  • Key aspects: Ancestral sequence reconstruction, disulfide bonds, salt bridges
  • Applications: Industrial process development, harsh condition tolerance

18. Space-Time Yield Optimization

  • Definition: Maximizing productivity per unit volume and time in biocatalytic processes
  • Key aspects: Substrate loading, catalyst concentration, reaction engineering
  • Applications: Industrial scale-up, economic viability assessment

19. Green Chemistry Integration

  • Definition: Incorporating biocatalysis into sustainable synthetic strategies
  • Key aspects: Renewable feedstocks, biodegradable catalysts, circular economy
  • Applications: Pharmaceutical manufacturing, sustainable chemical production

20. Substrate Scope vs. Selectivity Trade-offs

  • Definition: Balancing broad applicability with high stereoselectivity in enzyme design
  • Key aspects: Library screening, catalyst optimization, specificity engineering
  • Applications: Pharmaceutical development, process chemistry

21. Fed-Batch Process Strategies

  • Definition: Controlled substrate addition to optimize biocatalytic reactions
  • Key aspects: Substrate inhibition avoidance, equilibrium control, productivity enhancement
  • Applications: Industrial manufacturing, process intensification

22. Chemoenzymatic Synthesis

  • Definition: Integration of chemical and enzymatic steps in synthetic routes
  • Key aspects: Step compatibility, protection-deprotection avoidance, orthogonal reactivity
  • Applications: Natural product synthesis, API manufacturing

Winkler_2021_ACS_CentSci — Higher-order categories

  1. Why biocatalysis now (green-chemistry drivers & synthesis gaps). Motivation for routes that are cleaner, scalable, and cost-effective.

  2. Enzymes as precision catalysts. Active-site control, confined-space effects, dynamics/allostery — why enzymes deliver chemo/region/stereoselectivity.

  3. The “biocatalyst toolbox” for synthesis. Workhorse enzymatic transformations as a general category (hydrolysis, redox, C–C/CH-functionalization) rather than enumerated reactions.

  4. Tailoring catalysts (engineering & selection). Using directed evolution/rational design to tune scope, activity, and selectivity for target reactions.

  5. New-to-nature reactivity from enzyme promiscuity. Expanding beyond native function (e.g., engineered heme proteins for carbene/nitrene transfer).

  6. Photobiocatalysis as a radical-chemistry bridge. Light-enabled enzyme reactivity opening new bond-forming logic.

  7. Chemo–bio hybrids (protein-hosted small-molecule/metal catalysis). Artificial metalloenzymes and protein-embedded organocatalysts as a general platform.

  8. Multienzyme cascades & telescoped routes. One-pot/linear cascades for step-economic synthesis; showcased by the islatravir case.

  9. Process performance & implementation metrics. Emphasis that productivity metrics (e.g., STY) matter when translating to synthesis.

  10. Outlook: diversification, design, ML & training. Field needs: broader enzyme repertoires, computational/ML design, and integrating biocatalysis into chem curricula.
